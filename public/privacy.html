Privacy Policy

SendPulse Popups → Slack  ·  Last updated: May 2025

Who we are

This application ("SendPulse Popups → Slack") forwards lead submissions from SendPulse popup forms to Slack channels. It is an independent integration and is not affiliated with SendPulse or Slack Technologies.

Data we collect

We collect only the minimum data required to deliver the service:

  • Your SendPulse user ID and OAuth credentials — to authenticate with the SendPulse API.
  • Your Slack incoming webhook URL and channel name — to deliver notifications to your workspace.
  • Lead submissions received from your popup forms: visitor name, email address, and any other fields included in the form payload.
  • A delivery log per lead: timestamp, popup ID, and whether the Slack notification was delivered successfully.

How we use your data

All collected data is used exclusively to operate the integration — forwarding lead data from SendPulse to your configured Slack channel. We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties. We do not use it for advertising or analytics.

Data storage

Data is stored in an encrypted-at-rest SQLite database on a private server located in the EU. Slack webhook URLs and OAuth tokens are stored securely and are never exposed to third parties.

Cookies

We use a single session cookie to keep you authenticated inside the app. It is an HTTP-only, secure, same-site cookie and is not used for tracking.

Data retention and deletion

All data associated with your account — including credentials, webhook configuration, and lead logs — is permanently and automatically deleted the moment you uninstall the app from your SendPulse account. You may also request manual deletion at any time by contacting us.

Third-party services

The app communicates with the following external services:

  • SendPulse API (api.sendpulse.com) — to exchange OAuth tokens and receive popup events.
  • Slack API (hooks.slack.com) — to deliver lead notifications to your channel.

Each service is governed by its own privacy policy. We do not share data beyond what is required for these integrations to function.
